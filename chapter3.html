Chapter 3

Prototypes & Pragmatic Design

With the start of the product process being very much open we needed to figure out a general approach as to how to structure the work effectively. Initially we would utilize flat sketches mostly done through Sketch App (more on that later) and wireframes, but for us to try out things quickly, we realized that we needed to open up the design process.

A prototype is worth a thousand meetings.

Instead of talking around potential ideas, designs and hypotheticals we made a choice to make native prototypes. Through Xcode, and the newly updated Storyboards we managed to find an extremely rapid way of testing out big ideas. Me and Kasper working closely together. This gave us the possibility to paint broader strokes and try to circle in on a perimeter for what it was we were building. It also gave us the chance to get a feeling for the product, we could hold it in our hands, interact with gestures and show it to people to see how some of these ideas were being used. The prototypes helped us get a sense of direction, and through a series of elimination we managed to use these prototypes to figure out what we didn’t want, to eventually get to what we wanted. They became the center of discussion around where the product was going.

Through countless iterations we managed to finally start identifying some of the key problems that we were facing, but we also started to outline some of the experiences and features that had potential. Finding these potential solutions required us to work our way through numerous alternatives. While some of the prototypes definitely proved to be interesting, they eventually had to be cut in order for us to focus on telling a strong narrative, and simplifying, rather than adding new functionality to some of the experiences.

Example 2: Navigation

One of the elements that was an important part of our process was solving core Navigation. Our goal with the Navigation was to get it out of the way. What we wanted was to get away from things like a sidebar and standard Navigation patterns and focus purely on content. Our goal was to let the content be the driver between the experiences and not a sidebar or having to go through a stack to Navigate to a different experience.
